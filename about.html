About KOPA

Our Story

Kyanamukaaka Older People's Association (KOPA) is a community-based organization in Masaka District founded in May 2025. It is dedicated to improving the lives of senior citizens aged 55 and above through support, advocacy, and community programs.

We collaborate with families, local leaders, government bodies, and partners to ensure that older persons are included in social and economic development. Our programs positively impact over 500 elderly men and women in Kyanamukaaka Town Council and nearby areas.

Our Vision

To be a vibrant, inclusive, and empowered community of older persons aged 55 and above, actively contributing to national development and enjoying a high quality of life.

Our Mission

To improve the quality of life of older persons by providing support, promoting their rights, dignity, education, and income-generating opportunities through advocacy and partnerships.

Core Values

  • Respect
  • Integrity
  • Inclusiveness
  • Transparency
  • Empowerment
  • Accountability

Overall Goal

To empower older persons through healthcare, social protection, skills development, and advocacy for their rights and dignity.

Governance Structure

KOPA is governed by a General Assembly of all members and managed by a Board of Directors that includes the Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, General Secretary, Assistant Secretary (ICT Specialist), Treasurer, and various committee members. An Advisory Council of respected elders provides professional guidance and oversight.
